About This Project

Health care awareness programmes play an important role in educating people about diseases, hygiene, nutrition, and the importance of regular health check-ups. This mini project highlights some common health awareness programmes, shows their impact through simple statistics, and allows visitors to sign up to participate in upcoming awareness camps.

Awareness Programmes

  • Immunisation Awareness: Educates parents about vaccination schedules for children to prevent diseases like polio, measles, and hepatitis.
  • Hygiene and Sanitation: Promotes handwashing, clean drinking water, and proper waste disposal to reduce infections.
  • Maternal and Child Health: Spreads awareness about prenatal care, safe delivery, and infant nutrition.
  • Nutrition Awareness: Encourages balanced diets and addresses problems like anaemia and malnutrition.
  • Non-Communicable Disease Screening: Conducts free check-ups for blood pressure, diabetes, and other lifestyle diseases.
  • Mental Health Awareness: Reduces stigma around mental health and shares information about counselling services.

Impact of Awareness Programmes

Health Indicator Before Programme After Programme
Children fully immunised 42% 76%
Households practising handwashing 38% 61%
Pregnant women with regular check-ups 29% 48%
People aware of nearby health helpline 21% 67%

The data above shows that awareness programmes lead to a noticeable improvement in health-related knowledge and practices within just a few months.
